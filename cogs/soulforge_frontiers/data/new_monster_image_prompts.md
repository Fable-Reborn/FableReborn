# Soulforge Frontiers: New Wild Monster Image Prompts

These eight monsters were activated on 2026-07-16 after their final artwork was delivered and reviewed. This file remains the visual source of truth for replacements and future variants. Keep every design original; do not imitate or reference an existing franchise, artist, game, or copyrighted character.

## Shared direction for all eight images

Use this block at the end of every individual prompt:

> Original high-fantasy RPG creature concept art, full body, single creature, centered, readable silhouette, three-quarter view, dynamic but uncluttered pose, polished digital illustration, crisp edges, rich controlled color, detailed enough for a boss card but still clear as a small Discord embed, soft neutral studio lighting, transparent background, square 1024 x 1024 PNG. Keep the entire creature, horns, tail, wings, and effects inside the canvas with generous padding. No scenery, ground plane, border, frame, text, letters, symbols resembling text, logo, watermark, extra creatures, rider, saddle, weapon, or cropped anatomy.

If the generator cannot produce transparency, use a perfectly flat light-gray background with no cast shadow so it can be removed cleanly.

## 1. Galecrest Lynx — Wind

Filename suggestion: `galecrest_lynx.png`

Prompt:

> Design the Galecrest Lynx, a lean wildcat adapted to running across mountain wind currents. It has long athletic legs, a narrow feline face, large swept-back ears, and two short antler-like crests made from pale compressed cloud. Its fur is pearl gray fading into cool teal along the paws and tail. Layered tufts on its shoulders and flanks curl like elegant wind glyphs without forming written symbols. Its exceptionally long forked tail separates into two flowing vapor streamers. Show one forepaw lifted as if it has just stepped onto an invisible current, with a few restrained translucent air ribbons describing its movement. The creature should look alert, swift, clever, and naturally evolved rather than armored or mechanical.

Append the shared direction block.

## 2. Cloudray — Wind

Filename suggestion: `cloudray.png`

Prompt:

> Design the Cloudray, a large airborne manta-like creature that swims through the sky. Give it a broad diamond-shaped body, powerful curved wings, a small noble head, and a long ribbon tail. Its upper skin is deep storm blue with silver-white cloud marbling; its underside is pale cyan and softly luminous. Along the wing edges are thin translucent fins that resemble wisps of cirrus cloud. Several smooth floating air sacs follow the spine like natural buoyancy organs, not machinery. Pose it banking upward so both the wide silhouette and underside are readable. Add only a small wake of compressed mist curling from its wingtips. It should feel serene, ancient, aerodynamic, and unmistakably a creature rather than a vehicle.

## 3. Voltscale Pangolin — Electric

Filename suggestion: `voltscale_pangolin.png`

Prompt:

> Design the Voltscale Pangolin, a compact quadrupedal pangolin whose overlapping armor scales store electricity. Its scales are dark indigo edged in bright electric yellow, with subtle cyan light glowing between layers. Give it sturdy clawed feet, a long armored tail curling protectively around one side, and an expressive narrow face with bright blue eyes. A few scales along the shoulders are raised like charged capacitors, and small clean lightning arcs jump only between those raised scales. Pose it half-curled but ready to burst forward, preserving the recognizable pangolin silhouette. It should look tough, energetic, animalistic, and approachable enough to appear in early-game content.

## 4. Arcglass Mantis — Electric

Filename suggestion: `arcglass_mantis.png`

Prompt:

> Design the Arcglass Mantis, a tall predatory mantis with a narrow elegant body and enormous transparent scythe forelimbs. Its chitin is midnight blue and black with vivid violet joints; the forelimb blades look like naturally grown smoky crystal with branching golden lightning trapped inside. Give it four stable rear legs, a triangular head, bright white compound eyes, and two long antennae shaped by static into smooth arcs. Thin translucent wing cases sit folded along its back. Pose it upright with one crystal blade raised and the other guarding its body, surrounded by only a few controlled sparks. The anatomy must remain clearly insectoid, organic, dangerous, and regal—not humanoid and not robotic.

## 5. Tidereef Kelpie — Water

Filename suggestion: `tidereef_kelpie.png`

Prompt:

> Design the Tidereef Kelpie, an aquatic horse-like creature from a flooded forest. Its body is sleek sea-green fading to deep blue at the legs, with webbed hooves, small fin crests along the joints, and a long fish-like tail. Its mane is a living miniature reef made of soft branching coral, waving anemones, sea grass, and a few pearl-like growths; keep these features integrated into the animal rather than looking like objects placed on it. Give it calm luminous aqua eyes and small backward-curving coral horns. Pose it in a graceful rising trot with a thin spiral of water following the legs. It should feel protective and mysterious, with a strong equine silhouette and no rider or tack.

## 6. Bloomback Tortoise — Nature

Filename suggestion: `bloomback_tortoise.png`

Prompt:

> Design the Bloomback Tortoise, a gentle broad-bodied tortoise whose ancient shell supports a tiny flowering habitat. Its skin is warm moss green with earthy brown scales and kind amber eyes. The domed shell is dark bark and stone divided into natural plates; between the plates grow moss, clover, small ferns, and clusters of coral-pink and golden flowers. A single young sapling rises near the rear of the shell but stays short enough to keep the creature compact. Give its heavy feet root-like claws and pose it taking a patient forward step. The design should be charming without becoming cartoonish, sturdy enough to read as a defensive monster, and clean at thumbnail size.

## 7. Cinderhorn Ram — Fire

Filename suggestion: `cinderhorn_ram.png`

Prompt:

> Design the Cinderhorn Ram, a powerful mountain ram formed from soot-dark hide and volcanic stone. Give it a muscular low stance, heavy cloven hooves, a short ember-red mane, and two enormous symmetrical spiral horns made of cracked black basalt. Molten orange light glows through the horn cracks and in narrow lines across its shoulders, but most of the body remains solid and readable. Wisps of heat and a few floating cinders trail from the horns; avoid covering the body in uncontrolled flames. Pose it scraping one hoof and lowering its head just before a charge. It should look stubborn, physical, and dangerous, with a natural ram silhouette rather than a demon or armored mount.

Append the shared direction block.

## 8. Dawnveil Moth — Light

Filename suggestion: `dawnveil_moth.png`

Prompt:

> Design the Dawnveil Moth, a large celestial moth whose wings gather the first light of morning. Its soft body is ivory and pale gold with a plush collar, six delicate dark-gold legs, long feathered antennae, and calm opalescent eyes. The four broad wings transition from midnight violet at the lower edges through rose and amber into radiant cream near the top, like a sunrise. Each wing carries one large natural eyespot shaped like a softly glowing sun halo, with no letters or religious symbols. Long translucent veil-like tails flow from the lower wings and catch tiny motes of light. Pose it hovering with all four wings fully visible and symmetrical enough to read clearly, majestic and benevolent but still recognizably an insect.

## Delivery checklist

1. Keep the suggested filename or clearly state which monster it belongs to.
2. Supply the original 1024 x 1024 PNG rather than a screenshot or compressed preview.
3. Verify that no anatomy touches the canvas edge.
4. Verify that there is no text, logo, signature, or watermark.
5. If transparency is unavailable, leave the background perfectly flat and untextured.
6. For a future replacement, update the matching staging URL and repeat the review before changing its live `monsters.json` record.
